# Sections
sections:
  - id: overview
    icon: battery-charging
    content: |
      On 15 October 2025, the **National Development and Reform Commission (NDRC)** and other government agencies launched a three-year action plan to improve China&rsquo;s EV charging infrastructure. By end of 2027, China aims to establish a nationwide network of **28 million charging facilities**, with public charging capacity surpassing **300 million kilowatts** &mdash; sufficient to meet demand of more than 80 million EVs.

      Key measures include upgrading urban fast-charging networks, expediting charging facility upgrades in expressway service areas, and tackling rural charging infrastructure shortages. As of August 2025, total EV charging facilities stood at nearly **17.35 million units**, up 53.5% year-on-year.

      China&rsquo;s NEV parc hit 36.89 million units by end of June, accounting for 10.27% of total vehicles. Pure electric vehicles constituted 25.539 million units (69.23% of all NEVs).

  - id: timeline
    content: |
      **October 2025:** NDRC launches three-year action plan. **August 2025:** 17.35 million charging facilities nationwide. **2027 target:** 28 million facilities, 300 million kW public capacity. Location: **China (all areas)**, with measures for urban, expressway, and rural coverage.

  - id: stakeholders
    content: |

Infrastructure operators: **State Grid Corporation of China**, local government branches, **EVCIPA**. Private sector: **NaaS Technology/NewLink** (50% market share), Tesla, and others.

Data for siting of charging stations and cyber risk analysis.
