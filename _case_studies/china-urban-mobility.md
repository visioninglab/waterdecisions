# Sections
sections:
  - id: overview
    icon_emoji: "&#x1F688;"
    content: |
      Shahe Metro Station, a crucial node on the Changping Line, sees nearly **100,000 passengers daily**, with nearly 10,000 non-motorized vehicles parked around the station every day. This has led to long queues, disorderly parking, blocked fire lanes, sidewalks and motor vehicle lanes. The station serves approximately **150,000 residents**.

      The project creates a **three-story structure** following the design concept of separating pedestrians and vehicles, with one-way traffic. A &ldquo;sky bridge&rdquo; achieves seamless connection between the parking garage and subway station, enabling direct access from all floors &mdash; building a zero-distance transfer system of &ldquo;parking and entering the station.&rdquo;

  - id: timeline
    content: |
      **Q3 2025:** Construction officially commences. **Q1 2026:** Expected operational. Located at **Shahe subway station on the Changping Line, Beijing, China**.

  - id: stakeholders
    content: |
      **Beijing Municipal Development and Reform Commission**. Shahe municipal government. Local community residents, students at CUFE Shahe campus. EV charging infrastructure providers and bicycle sharing companies.

  - id: digitalisation
    content: |
      The project developed the city&rsquo;s **first full-process management APP** for non-motorized vehicles. It connects cameras, temperature sensors, AI, and a visualization screen to overcome misjudgment in dense traffic. Features: seamless passage, entry/exit records, intelligent parking and retrieval, real-time illegal parking warnings, and fire risk monitoring.

      The APP uses multiple data including **vehicle ID, parking behavior, and user credit**.
