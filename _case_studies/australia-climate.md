# Sections
sections:
  - id: overview
    icon_emoji: "&#x1F30D;"
    content: |
      The **National Climate Resilience and Adaptation Strategy (NCRAS)** was set out by the Department of Agriculture, Water and the Environment, with scope across four domains: natural, built, social and economic (2021). The **Australia Climate Service (ACS)** was established in 2021 as a partnership between the Bureau of Meteorology, CSIRO, Australian Bureau of Statistics, and Geoscience Australia.

      Insurance claims average **AUS$4.5 billion each year** (three times 1990 figures). Three events in 2025 alone generated nearly $2 billion in claims. The Insurance Catastrophe Resilience Report 2024&ndash;25 reported the financial cost of insured extreme weather over five years at around **$22.5 billion**, a 67% jump from the previous five-year period.

      Insured losses are just over half of all losses, meaning businesses, communities and the government are bearing around AUS$4.5 billion in additional losses each year due to weather events.

  - id: timeline
    content: |
      **2019:** Australian Government invested $3.9 billion into the Future Drought Fund. **2021:** NCRAS established; ACS created; Preparing Australia Communities Program invested A$600 million. **2022:** DCCEEW created. **2024:** National Climate Risk Assessment First Pass Assessment Report.

      Location: **Australia (all territories)**.

  - id: stakeholders
    content: |
      ACS is a partnership between **Bureau of Meteorology** (accountable authority), **CSIRO**, **Australian Bureau of Statistics**, and **Geoscience Australia**. The **DCCEEW** also contributes to the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC).
