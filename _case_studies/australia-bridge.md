# Sections
sections:
  - id: overview
    icon: waves
    content: |
      The Fitzroy River Bridge, in addition to 500 metres of road, was significantly damaged following historical flooding through the Kimberley Region early in 2023. Ex-tropical cyclone Ellie damaged the highway and caused the bridge to collapse in December 2022.

      The **New Fitzroy River Bridge** was designed and constructed in just **10 months** — six months ahead of schedule — and is **six times stronger** than the original. It reconnects families and communities on the east and west sides of the river and reinstates essential access for Australia's freight and transport industries.

  - id: timeline
    content: |
      Ex-tropical cyclone Ellie caused the bridge collapse in **December 2022**. The New Fitzroy River Bridge was designed and constructed in 10 months and opened in **December 2023**, one year after the cyclone — six months ahead of schedule.

      Located in the **Kimberley region of Western Australia**.

  - id: stakeholders
    content: |
      Owned by the **Western Australian government** and the **Australian federal government**. Built by an alliance between **Georgiou, BMD Group, BG&E and Main Roads** (Western Australia government). Users include government, architects, engineers, constructors, and operators.

  - id: digitalisation
    content: |
      **Flood modelling tools** were used in the design process. New data from the 2023 floods was collected and incorporated into flood modelling to ensure up-to-date information and advice about future flood risks.
