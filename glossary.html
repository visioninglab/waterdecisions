A

Adaptively Transforming (P5)
One of the six UNDRR Principles of Resilient Infrastructure. Adapting and transforming to changing needs through manageable solutions, adaptive capacity, flexible management, and allowing for human discretion in decision-making. See: Principles | China Hydraulic, Singapore DTSS
AECOM
Global infrastructure consulting firm; joint venture partner overseeing Singapore DTSS Phase 2. See: Singapore DTSS

B

Black & Veatch
Engineering firm; joint venture partner with AECOM on Singapore DTSS Phase 2. See: Singapore DTSS

C

Continually Learning and Improving (P6)
One of the six UNDRR Principles of Resilient Infrastructure. Developing and updating understanding and insight into infrastructure resilience by exposing and validating assumptions, monitoring and intervening appropriately, analysing and learning from events, and conducting stress tests. See: Principles | China Hydraulic, Singapore DTSS

D

DIR — Design for Infrastructure Resilience
The research framework underlying this wiki, focusing on how infrastructure can be designed, built, and managed to withstand and recover from disruptions.
Digital Twin
A virtual replica of a physical system that receives real-time data for monitoring, simulation, and predictive analytics. Enables scenario modelling and early warning. See: Singapore DTSS
DTSS — Deep Tunnel Sewerage System
Singapore’s 206 km underground used-water superhighway connecting to three water reclamation plants. A national-scale infrastructure system with a 100-year design life. See: Singapore DTSS

E

Environmentally Integrated (P3)
One of the six UNDRR Principles of Resilient Infrastructure. Working in a positively integrated way with the natural environment by minimising environmental impact, using environmental solutions, integrating ecosystem information, maintaining natural systems, and using local sustainable resources. See: Principles | China Hydraulic, Singapore DTSS

G

Green Infrastructure
Natural or semi-natural systems that provide environmental services such as stormwater management, urban cooling, and flood attenuation. Examples include rain gardens, bioswales, permeable pavements, and urban forests. See: China Hydraulic

H

HDPE — High-Density Polyethylene
Corrosion-resistant polymer material used for tunnel linings in wastewater infrastructure to withstand chemical exposure over long design lives. See: Singapore DTSS

I

ISO 22372:2025
International standard: Security and resilience — Community resilience — Guidelines for infrastructure resilience. Developed by ISO/TC 292 with the contribution of UNDRR. The first global standard for infrastructure resilience, built on the six UNDRR Principles for Resilient Infrastructure (P1–P6). Provides practical guidance for planning, maintaining, and improving infrastructure resilience across sectors and borders. See: ISO 22372:2025 | Principles, Assessment
IWMF — Integrated Waste Management Facility
Facility at Tuas, Singapore, integrating used water treatment with solid waste processing for resource recovery. See: Singapore DTSS

N

NEWater
Singapore’s brand of high-grade reclaimed water, purified from treated used water through advanced membrane and UV technologies. Supplies approximately 40% of the nation’s water needs, projected to increase to 55%. See: Singapore DTSS

P

Proactively Protected (P2)
One of the six UNDRR Principles of Resilient Infrastructure. Proactively planning, designing, building and operating infrastructure prepared for current and future hazards, with raised safety requirements, consideration of complex interdependencies, and commitment to long-term maintenance and investment. See: Principles | China Hydraulic, Singapore DTSS
PUB — Public Utilities Board
Singapore’s National Water Agency, responsible for the collection, production, distribution, and reclamation of water. Owner and operator of the DTSS. See: Singapore DTSS

R

Resilience
The ability of infrastructure to anticipate, absorb, adapt to, and rapidly recover from disruptive events while maintaining essential functions. The central concept of the DIR framework.

S

SCADA — Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition
Industrial control system architecture for monitoring and controlling infrastructure processes such as water treatment, power generation, and transport systems. See: Singapore DTSS
Clearly Defined Accountabilities and Shared Responsibilities (P1)
One of the six UNDRR Principles of Resilient Infrastructure. Sharing information and expertise for coordinated benefits through harmonised open standards, collaborative management, clear accountability, enhanced connectivity for information sharing, data safety, and transparent risk-and-return information. See: Principles | China Hydraulic, Singapore DTSS
Socially Engaged (P4)
One of the six UNDRR Principles of Resilient Infrastructure. Developing active engagement, involvement and participation across all levels of society by informing people about disruptions, raising resilience literacy, and encouraging community participation. See: Principles | China Hydraulic, Singapore DTSS

T

TBM — Tunnel Boring Machine
Heavy mechanical equipment used to excavate tunnels through soil and rock. Used extensively in Singapore DTSS construction for the deep sewer tunnels. See: Singapore DTSS

U

UNDRR — United Nations Office for Disaster Risk Reduction
UN body responsible for coordinating efforts to reduce disaster risk worldwide. Publisher of the Handbook for Implementing the Principles for Resilient Infrastructure, which defines the six principles (P1–P6) used throughout this wiki. Contributed to the development of ISO 22372:2025. See: UNDRR Handbook | Principles, Assessment
UNESCO — UN Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization
UN agency that designated the Dujiangyan Irrigation System as a World Heritage Site in 2000, recognising its outstanding universal value as ancient hydraulic engineering. See: China Hydraulic

W

WRP — Water Reclamation Plant
Facility for treating used water to produce reclaimed water (NEWater) and for safe discharge. Singapore’s three WRPs (Changi, Kranji, Tuas) are connected by the DTSS network. See: Singapore DTSS
