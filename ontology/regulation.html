Definition

Legal frameworks, standards, and regulatory instruments that govern how water infrastructure is planned, built, operated, and monitored. These shape the decision space within which engineers, operators, and policymakers act.

Key Concepts

Water Framework Directive (WFD)
EU legislation requiring member states to achieve good ecological status for all water bodies.
Environment Act 2021
UK legislation placing duties on water companies to reduce sewage discharges and monitor water quality.
Drinking Water Directive
EU standards for the quality of water intended for human consumption.
ISO 22372
International standard for security and resilience of infrastructure, providing principles for resilient infrastructure.
Bathing Water Directive
EU legislation setting quality standards for designated bathing waters.
Flood Risk Regulations
Transposition of the EU Floods Directive requiring assessment, mapping, and management of flood risks.
