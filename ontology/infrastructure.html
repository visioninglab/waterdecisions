Definition

Physical assets, networks, and engineered systems that capture, store, treat, distribute, and discharge water. They range from ancient gravity-fed channels to modern deep-tunnel sewerage networks and smart pipe systems.

Key Concepts

Combined Sewer Overflow (CSO)
Combined sewer systems carry stormwater and wastewater together. During heavy rain, excess flow is discharged — often untreated — into rivers or the sea.
Deep Tunnel Sewerage
Large-diameter tunnels built at depth to convey wastewater by gravity to centralised treatment plants, reducing surface-level infrastructure.
Water Reclamation Plant (WRP)
Facilities that treat used water to a standard suitable for reuse, including potable reuse (e.g. NEWater).
Flood Defence
Engineered barriers, embankments, and movable structures designed to protect communities from fluvial or coastal flooding.
Irrigation Network
Channels, weirs, and control structures that divert and distribute water for agricultural use.
Desalination Plant
Facilities that convert seawater or brackish water into potable water, typically using reverse osmosis.
