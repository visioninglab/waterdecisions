Definition

Natural and human-caused threats to water systems. These hazards drive the need for resilient infrastructure, monitoring, and adaptive decision-making.

Key Concepts

Fluvial Flooding
Flooding caused by rivers and watercourses exceeding their capacity, inundating surrounding land.
Pluvial Flooding
Surface water flooding from rainfall that overwhelms drainage systems before reaching a watercourse.
Combined Sewer Overflow
Discharge of untreated sewage and stormwater during heavy rainfall events when combined sewers exceed capacity.
Drought
Extended periods of below-average precipitation leading to water supply shortages and ecosystem stress.
Water Pollution
Contamination of water bodies by chemical, biological, or physical agents from point or diffuse sources.
Climate Change
Long-term shifts in temperature and weather patterns affecting water availability, flood frequency, and infrastructure resilience.
