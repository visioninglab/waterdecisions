Definition

Sensors, telemetry, digital twins, and data systems used to observe, measure, and manage water infrastructure in real time and over the long term.

Key Concepts

SCADA
Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition — industrial control systems for remote monitoring and automation of water infrastructure.
Digital Twin
A virtual replica of a physical water system, fed by real-time sensor data, used for simulation and decision support.
Telemetry
Remote measurement and transmission of data from sensors in the field to central control systems.
Water Quality Sensor
Instruments measuring parameters such as pH, dissolved oxygen, turbidity, and conductivity in real time.
Flow Meter
Devices measuring the rate of water or wastewater flow through pipes, channels, or rivers.
Event Detection System
Automated systems that identify anomalous readings — pollution spikes, pipe bursts, or overflow events.
